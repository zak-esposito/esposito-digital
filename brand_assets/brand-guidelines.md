# Zak Esposito — Personal Portfolio Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity
- **Name on site:** Zak Esposito (primary), Esposito Digital (secondary/business name)
- **Site header/nav:** "Z.E" or "Zak Esposito" — NOT "Esposito Digital" as the main branding
- **Tone:** Personal, confident, direct, slightly playful but not cringe. Like talking to a smart friend who happens to build software.

## Colour Palette (Dark — canonical)
- Background Base: #07090A (page base, behind everything)
- Background Body: #07090A (body surface)
- Accent: #10B981 (emerald green — the single accent, used sparingly for emphasis, focus, and CTA)
- Text Primary: #E8EBED (off-white for body and headings)

The accent is the only chromatic colour in the system. Everything else is neutral on dark. Do not introduce a secondary accent (no indigo, no purple, no blue) — the green has to carry all emphasis on its own.

## Typography
- Headings: Bold, characterful display font (700–800 weight)
- Body: Clean readable sans (400 weight)
- Hero name: Extra large, confident sizing
- Heading tracking: tight (-0.03em)
- Body line-height: 1.7

## Personality Touches (subtle, not forced)
- The green accent ties to personal taste (favourite colour) and is the through-line that keeps the site recognisably "Zak"
- Copy should sound like Zak talking, not a brochure
- Can include a small motto or personal tagline: "Build fast. Learn deeply. Ship real things."

## What This Site Should Communicate
- "This person is smart, modern, credible, and capable"
- "They have real engineering experience, not just tutorials"
- "They're approachable and easy to work with"
- "They build things that actually work"

## What To AVOID
- Corporate/agency language ("we transform your business")
- AI hype language ("cutting-edge revolutionary AI")
- Fake testimonials or filler content
- Over-animation or gimmicky effects
- Generic Tailwind blue/indigo
- Looking like every other dark-mode dev portfolio — the racing-menu theme is what differentiates this from the default
