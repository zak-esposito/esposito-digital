# Zak Esposito — Personal Portfolio Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity
- **Name on site:** Zak Esposito (primary), Esposito Digital (secondary/business name)
- **Site header/nav:** "Z.E" or "Zak Esposito" — NOT "Esposito Digital" as the main branding
- **Tone:** Personal, confident, direct, slightly playful but not cringe. Like talking to a smart friend who happens to build software.

## Colour Palette
- Background Primary: #FAFAFA (off-white, clean and light — NOT dark theme this time)
- Background Secondary: #FFFFFF (pure white for cards/elevated surfaces)
- Background Dark Sections: #0A0A0A (for contrast sections, like the about/intro block)
- Accent Primary: #10B981 (emerald green — vibrant, energetic, modern)
- Accent Hover: #059669 (darker emerald on hover)
- Accent Secondary: #6366F1 (indigo/purple — for secondary highlights, like Rulian's purple shape)
- Text Primary: #111827 (near-black for headings on light backgrounds)
- Text Secondary: #6B7280 (grey for body text)
- Text On Dark: #F5F5F5 (off-white for text on dark sections)
- Accent Glow: rgba(16, 185, 129, 0.15) (subtle green glow for effects)

## Typography
- Headings: Bold, characterful display font — try "Outfit", "Plus Jakarta Sans", or "Cabinet Grotesk" (700-800 weight)
- Body: Clean readable sans — "DM Sans" or "Geist" (400 weight)
- Hero name: Extra large, confident sizing (4-5rem+)
- Name accent: The first name "Zak" or a key word in green to add personality
- Heading tracking: tight (-0.03em)
- Body line-height: 1.7

## Layout Reference
Based on rulian.co structure:
1. **Nav** — minimal. Left: "Z.E" or initials. Right: "What I Do" / "My Work" / "Contact" + green CTA button
2. **Hero** — large "Hello! I'm Zak" with subtitle "Developer, Builder & Problem Solver" (or similar). Photo of Zak prominently placed. Green CTA button below.
3. **Value prop** — short punchy statement: "I help businesses get online and run smarter." with 1-2 lines of supporting text
4. **About/Intro** — dark background section. Split layout with photo and text. Who Zak is, background, what he builds. Mention Cardiff Uni, Renishaw, real engineering experience.
5. **What I Do** — services section. Websites, Web Apps, AI Automations. Clean cards or list format.
6. **My Work** — project cards with screenshots/placeholders. Snap2Shop, VentureForge, Bakehouse 72 concept. Each card shows: screenshot, title, short description, tech tags.
7. **Contact/CTA** — "Got a project in mind?" with email link and/or simple form. Social links (GitHub, LinkedIn).
8. **Footer** — minimal. Copyright, social icons.

## Photo
- Use Zak's real photo (from LinkedIn or similar)
- Style it with a fun background shape (like Rulian's purple blob) but in green/emerald
- Photo should feel approachable and confident, not corporate

## Personality Touches (subtle, not forced)
- The green accent colour ties to personal taste (favourite colour)
- Copy should sound like Zak talking, not a brochure
- Can include a small motto or personal tagline in the footer: "Build fast. Learn deeply. Ship real things."
- Keep it clean and athletic feeling — not cluttered, not over-designed

## What This Site Should Communicate
- "This person is smart, modern, credible, and capable"
- "They have real engineering experience, not just tutorials"
- "They're approachable and easy to work with"
- "They build things that actually work"

## What To AVOID
- Corporate/agency language ("we transform your business")
- AI hype language ("cutting-edge revolutionary AI")
- Fake testimonials or filler content
- Over-animation or gimmicky effects
- Dark theme (we're going light this time for a fresh feel)
- Generic Tailwind blue/indigo
- Looking like every other dark-mode dev portfolio

## Tech Stack References (for display on site)
Show these as subtle tech labels or icons:
React, TypeScript, C#, Node.js, Python, SQL, Tailwind CSS, AI/LLMs
